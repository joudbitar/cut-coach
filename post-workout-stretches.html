Post-Leg Day Recovery Stretches

After squats + RDLs · 45–60 sec each side · No bouncing

1

Standing Quad Stretch

Quadriceps
Hold: 45–60 sec / side

Pull ankle to glute, keep knees together. Hold a wall if you wobble. Feel it deep in the front of the thigh.

2

Hip Flexor Lunge Stretch

Hip Flexors · Psoas
Hold: 45–60 sec / side

Back knee on floor, sink hips forward and down. Keep torso tall. You'll feel this at the front of the back hip.

3

Standing Forward Fold

Hamstrings · Lower Back
Hold: 45–60 sec

Feet hip-width, soft knees. Let gravity do the work — just hang. Decompresses spine and releases hamstrings passively.

4

Figure-4 Stretch

Glutes · Piriformis
Hold: 45–60 sec / side

Cross ankle over opposite knee. Sit up tall or lean forward for more intensity. Deep glute burn = good.

5

Supine Hamstring Stretch

Hamstrings
Hold: 45–60 sec / side

Lie on back, loop towel/belt around foot, pull leg toward you. Keep the other leg flat. Breathe into it — the leg will creep higher.

6

Child's Pose

Lower Back · Hips · Lats
Hold: 60 sec

Wide knees, big toes touching, arms reach forward. Full exhale on every breath — let the hips sink heavier into the heels each time.

7

Legs Up the Wall

Passive Recovery · Circulation
Hold: 2–3 min

Sit sideways against wall, swing legs up. Totally passive. Gravity drains lactic acid from the legs back toward the heart. The most underrated recovery move — don't skip this one.

Do in order · No bouncing · Breathe through each hold
